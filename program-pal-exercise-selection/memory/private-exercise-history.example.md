## Example Entry

### Goblet Squat

Source Status: Verified

Source Link Or Note: ACE/NASM-style common squat variation; verify source link when needed.

Category: Strength

Movement Pattern: Squat

Placement: Workout

Equipment: Dumbbell or kettlebell

Difficulty: Beginner to intermediate

Used For:

- Squat-pattern development
- Lower-body strength
- Teaching bracing and depth control

Best For:

- Clients learning squat mechanics
- Clients needing controlled squat loading
- Clients progressing toward heavier squat variations

Use Caution When:

- Knee pain
- Low-back discomfort
- Poor bracing
- Limited ankle mobility

Regression:

- Box squat
- Assisted squat
- Bodyweight squat to target

Progression:

- Tempo goblet squat
- Double kettlebell front squat
- Front squat

Substitutions:

- Leg press
- Split squat
- Dumbbell front squat

Coaching Notes:

- Keep ribs stacked over pelvis.
- Brace before descending.
- Control depth instead of chasing depth at all costs.

Programming Notes:

- Useful when the goal is squat quality without jumping straight to barbell loading.

Successful Uses:

- Beginner lower-body strength client.
- Movement-quality block focused on squat depth.

Outcome Notes:

- Worked well when paired with ankle and hip mobility in warm-up.
