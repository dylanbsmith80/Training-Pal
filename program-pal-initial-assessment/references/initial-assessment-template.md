# Initial Assessment Template Reference

This reference captures the structure of the Program Pal Fitness Assessment template. Use it to understand what information may appear in a completed or partially completed assessment.

## Document Structure

The template is a seven-page personal training initial assessment form originally structured for an initial client session.

## Client Metadata

- Client name
- Age
- Gender
- Date
- Personal trainer

## Medical History

- Other conditions or health concerns not listed on intake forms
- Medications and supplements
  - Name
  - Reason
  - Dosage
  - Frequency
- Pain locations and pain notes

## Activity History

The form references general adult activity recommendations:

- 150 to 300 minutes per week of moderate-intensity aerobic activity, or
- 75 to 150 minutes per week of vigorous-intensity aerobic activity, or
- An equivalent mix of moderate and vigorous activity
- Muscle-strengthening activities involving all major muscle groups 2 or more days per week

Fields:

- Days per week of moderate to vigorous activity
- Minutes per activity session
- Total minutes per week
- Days per week of muscle-strengthening exercise
- Current regular physical activity: yes/no
- Past physical activity if not currently active: yes/no
- Cardiorespiratory exercise types, frequency, current or past
- Resistance-training types, frequency, current or past
- Average hours sitting per day
- Stress level: high, moderate, or low
- Stress-management strategies

## Nutrition

- Weight changes in the last six months
  - Gain amount
  - Loss amount
- Eating-habit rating from 5 very healthy to 1 very unhealthy
- Meals per day
- Snacks per day
- Whether the client normally eats breakfast
- Typical breakfast if applicable
- Whether the client goes long periods without eating
- Average duration without eating if applicable
- Average water intake in ounces per day
- Current or past consistent smoking
- Smoking frequency if applicable
- Nutritional concerns

## Body Composition

- Height
- Weight
- Resting heart rate
- Sitting blood pressure
- Standing blood pressure

Blood pressure categories listed in the template:

- Normal: less than 120/80
- Elevated: 120-129 / less than 80
- Hypertension Stage 1: 130-139 / 80-89
- Hypertension Stage 2: greater than 140 / greater than 90
- Hypertensive crisis: greater than 180 / greater than 120

Skinfold/body composition fields:

- Triceps, female
- Suprailium, female
- Thigh
- Abdomen, male
- Chest, male
- Sum
- Body fat composition and classification
- Body mass index and classification
- Waist/hip ratio and classification

Measurements:

- Chest
- Waist
- Hips
- Left and right biceps
- Left and right thigh
- Left and right calf

InBody fields:

- Body fat composition and classification
- Body mass index and classification

## Cardiovascular Endurance

- Step test and classification
- One-mile walk and classification
- 1.5-mile walk/run and classification

## Muscular Endurance And Strength

- Curl-ups and classification
- Push-ups and classification
- Pull-up or arm-hang and classification
- 1RM squat
- Plank
- Left plank
- Right plank
- Other tests

## FMS

- Deep squat
  - Any pain
  - Score
- Shoulder mobility
  - Clearing test right and left
  - Score right and left
- Active straight leg raise
  - Any pain
  - Score right and left
- Hurdle step
  - Any pain
  - Score right and left
- In-line lunge
  - Any pain
  - Score right and left
- Trunk stability push-up
  - Clearing test
  - Score right and left

## Additional Assessments

- Open notes section for extra testing or observations

## Posture

- Posture notes

## SMART Goals

- Specific: what the client wants to achieve and why
- Measurable: how progress will be measured
- Attainable: whether the goal is practical
- Relevant: whether the goal is realistic with available resources and time
- Time-bound: target date for reaching the goal

## Fitness Goals

- Exercises the client likes
- Exercises the client dislikes
- Long-term fitness goals
- Short-term fitness goals
- What the client wants from a personal trainer
- What the client wants from an exercise program
- Other notes
